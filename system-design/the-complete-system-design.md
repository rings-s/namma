## The Operating System for Service Commerce in the Gulf
### Master Business & Platform Documentation — Investor & Partner Edition (v2.0, June 2026)
>  **One line:** Namaa is the compliance-native, AI-driven operating system that runs every revenue-generating minute of a Gulf service business — bookings, POS, payments, staff, marketing, and ZATCA e-invoicing — in one Arabic-first platform. 

---

# 1. Executive Summary
Service businesses are the fastest-growing commercial segment of Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 economy — clinics, salons, spas, fitness studios, and the entertainment venues created by an unprecedented national push into leisure and tourism. Yet the average multi-branch operator in Riyadh still runs the business on a patchwork of seven disconnected tools: a booking app, a cashier program, WhatsApp on a personal phone, Excel for commissions, a separate ZATCA invoicing utility, paper consent forms, and gut feeling for marketing.

**Namaa replaces all of it.** One platform, built in Saudi Arabia for the Gulf, that unifies multi-branch operations, intelligent CRM, automated omnichannel engagement, real-time POS with regional payments, and Phase-2 ZATCA e-invoicing — with an AI layer that turns daily operational data into revenue decisions.

Our wedge is unique in this market: **compliance is the Trojan horse, payments are the engine, AI is the moat.** Every Saudi service business is legally compelled toward Phase-2 e-invoicing; Namaa makes compliance a one-click property of normal selling rather than a separate burden. Once we run the invoice, we run the payment — earning a platform margin on every card transaction. And because we see bookings, payments, inventory, and engagement in one schema, our AI predictions (churn, demand, staffing) work out of the box in ways point-solutions structurally cannot match.

**The business model** combines high-retention SaaS subscriptions (449 → 3,500+ SAR/month across four tiers) with usage-based revenue (payments margin, messaging margin, AI add-ons) — a model designed for net revenue retention above 110% as customers open branches and adopt modules.

**We are raising seed funding** to complete the platform build, achieve ZATCA certification, acquire our first 100 paying customers in Riyadh and Jeddah, and reach a 3.5M+ SAR ARR run rate within 12 months of launch (detailed plan in §9–10).

---

# 2. The Problem
A typical 3-branch clinic or salon group in KSA today:

1. **Loses 25–40% of potential bookings** because booking happens by phone during working hours only, and double-bookings of rooms/devices are prevented by a paper diary.
2. **Suffers chronic no-shows** with no deposit enforcement and manual reminder calls.
3. **Pays staff commissions calculated in Excel** — slow, disputed, and demotivating.
4. **Faces ZATCA Phase-2 enforcement waves** with invoicing tools that don't talk to the cashier screen, risking fines and operational chaos.
5. **Owns zero customer intelligence:** no LTV, no churn signal, no segmentation — marketing is a blast SMS to everyone, in violation of PDPL consent rules they've never heard of.
6. **Cannot see the business in real time:** the owner learns branch performance days later, from a WhatsApp photo of a handwritten summary.
International tools (Fresha, Zenoti, Booker) are English-first, compliance-blind to ZATCA/PDPL, and price in dollars with no local support. Local tools solve one slice (booking _or_ cashier _or_ invoicing) and force the patchwork anyway. **Nobody owns the full operating layer. Namaa does.**

---

# 3. The Market
**Why now — three converging forces:**

- **Regulatory forcing function:** ZATCA's Phase-2 integration waves are progressively compelling every VAT-registered business into certified, cryptographically-stamped e-invoicing. This converts "software is nice to have" into "software is the law."
- **Vision 2030 service-economy boom:** entertainment licensing (GEA), tourism targets, gym/wellness expansion, and clinic privatization are creating tens of thousands of new venues — all born digital-ready, none served by an integrated local platform.
- **Payment digitization:** mada, Apple Pay, and SoftPOS adoption make embedded payments — our highest-margin layer — frictionless to attach.
**Sizing method (bottom-up, internal estimates to be validated against GASTAT/MISA registries during diligence):** KSA alone hosts well over 100,000 VAT-relevant service venues across salons/barbershops, clinics (dental, derma, physio), spas/wellness, fitness, and entertainment. At a blended 1,000–1,400 SAR/month software + payments revenue per venue, the Saudi serviceable market alone exceeds **1.5B SAR annually**, before GCC expansion (UAE e-invoicing arrives next, and our compliance engine transfers directly).

**Beachhead:** multi-chair salons/barbershops and dental/derma clinics in Riyadh and Jeddah — highest density, fastest sales cycles, strongest compliance pain. Entertainment/ticketing follows as the second wave (§10).

---

# 4. The Product — What Makes Namaa Different
Four compounding differentiators, in order of defensibility:

1. **Compliance-native core.** ZATCA Phase 2 (cryptographic stamps, PIH hash chains, ICV counters, device CSID onboarding, clearance & reporting flows) is built into the invoice lifecycle itself — not bolted on. PDPL consent management is built into the CRM. This is 12–18 months of regulatory engineering that global competitors will not do and local point-tools cannot generalize.
2. **Embedded payments.** Moyasar-powered acceptance (mada, Visa/Mastercard, Apple Pay) wired directly into POS, deposits, gift cards, and online booking — with Namaa earning a platform margin on volume. Software subscriptions get us in the door; payments scale our revenue with the customer's success.
3. **One schema → real AI.** Because bookings, sales, inventory, staff, and messaging live in one data model per tenant, our AI layer (LTV prediction, churn early-warning, demand forecasting, smart staffing, Arabic marketing-copy generation) trains on complete behavioral pictures — the structural advantage no patchwork can replicate.
4. **Arabic-first, Gulf-first.** True RTL interfaces, bilingual invoices and messaging, Hijri-aware scheduling, prayer-time and Ramadan operating-hour intelligence, GCC currencies and timezones, and in-Kingdom data residency options.

# 5. Complete Feature Catalog (Consolidated)
_Impact figures below are category benchmark outcomes Namaa is engineered to deliver and will be validated with design-partner data; they are targets, not audited results._

## 5.1 Organization & Multi-Branch Control
Multi-organization management under one account; branch-specific hours, pricing, services, and staff; industry templates (clinic, salon, spa, fitness, entertainment); GCC timezones and currencies (SAR, AED, KWD, BHD, OMR, QAR); geo-located branch finder; consolidated reporting with branch drill-down. _Target impact: ~60% reduction in multi-location administrative overhead._

## 5.2 Staff, Scheduling & Compensation
Rich employee profiles with certifications and documents; branch- and function-scoped RBAC permissions; qualification-linked service assignments; flexible shift templates with breaks, time-off workflows, and holiday exceptions; commission engine (percentage, fixed, tiered, per-service or per-product) with automated real-time calculation; payroll periods with multi-step approval and export. _Targets: ~75% fewer scheduling conflicts; ~80% lighter HR admin burden._

## 5.3 CRM & Customer Intelligence
360° customer profiles (visit history, notes by category, secure documents, communication log, custom fields); AI-computed LTV, churn probability, and next-visit prediction; dynamic, AI-generated, and manual segments with real-time refresh; NPS and post-visit surveys with sentiment analysis; PDPL-compliant consent and channel preferences. _Targets: ~40% better personalization; ~25% churn reduction._

## 5.4 Booking, Waitlist & Queue Engine
24/7 self-service online booking with live availability across the staff + room + equipment triad and database-level double-booking prevention; multi-service sessions; drag-and-drop calendars; buffer times; smart waitlists with VIP priority; walk-in virtual queues via QR/kiosk with live wait estimates; multi-touch reminders (SMS/WhatsApp/email/push) with two-way confirmation; deposit and cancellation-policy enforcement. _Targets: ~35% more bookings; ~50% fewer no-shows; ~20% better slot utilization._

## 5.5 Events & Ticketing
Classes, workshops, and event series with capacity control; tiered ticket types (standard/VIP/early-bird) with dynamic pricing; QR-secured digital tickets with mobile scan verification, multi-entry passes, and full check-in audit trail. _Targets: new revenue stream; ~70% faster entry; ~90% less ticket fraud._

## 5.6 Catalog, Packages & Dynamic Pricing
Service and retail product catalogs with cost tracking and SKU management; bundles, memberships, and prepaid packages with validity and usage controls; time-, demand-, and segment-based dynamic pricing; promotional overrides. _Targets: ~45% higher average transaction value; ~15% revenue lift from pricing optimization._

## 5.7 POS, Payments & Stored Value
Unified cart (services + products + packages + gift cards) in one transaction; split and partial payments, tips, deposits (refundable/non-refundable), full/partial refunds; Moyasar gateway integration (mada, Visa/Mastercard, Apple Pay) with real-time webhooks; digital and physical gift cards and store credit, all posted to an immutable double-entry ledger for audit-grade financial integrity. _Targets: ~25% faster checkout; ~80% less billing admin._

## 5.8 ZATCA E-Invoicing (Phase 2)
Per-device CSID onboarding; UBL 2.1 XML generation; cryptographic stamping, PIH chaining, and ICV sequencing; B2B clearance and B2C reporting flows; credit/debit notes with reason codes; QR TLV on every invoice; asynchronous submission with retry — invisible to the cashier, bulletproof for the auditor.

## 5.9 Inventory & Procurement
Multi-location stock with append-only movement ledger; automatic sale deduction; transfers, adjustments, waste logging; FIFO/LIFO valuation; reorder alerts; supplier database and purchase orders with partial receiving. _Targets: ~40% fewer stockouts; ~30% less excess inventory._

## 5.10 Marketing, Loyalty & Referrals
Segment-targeted multi-channel campaigns (SMS, WhatsApp, email, push) with A/B testing and ROI tracking; discount codes with usage and threshold rules; tiered points-based loyalty (earning rules, expiry, full ledger); dual-sided referral programs with fraud validation; automated lifecycle journeys (reminders, win-back, birthdays, milestones). _Targets: ~3x campaign ROI; ~25% retention lift; ~20% of new customers via referral._

## 5.11 Unified Communication Hub
One inbox for SMS, WhatsApp Business, email, push, and in-app threads; approved multi-language template library with variable substitution; conversation routing to branch staff with status workflow; delivery/read tracking and per-message cost accounting (re-billable). _Targets: ~95% reach rate; ~40% faster response times._

## 5.12 Analytics, BI & Goals
Real-time role-specific dashboards; operational, financial, customer, and marketing report suites; branch benchmarking; daily aggregated metrics with anomaly detection; multi-level goal setting (org/branch/individual) with milestones, rewards, and leaderboards.

## 5.13 AI Suite
Demand and revenue forecasting; churn and LTV models; smart staff allocation; Arabic/English marketing copy and service-description generation; conversational AI assistant answering natural-language business questions on the tenant's own data; AI report summaries. _Targets: ~50% less analysis time; ~25% better resource planning._

## 5.14 Forms, Documents & Clinical Records
Drag-and-drop form builder (intake, consent, medical history, feedback) with conditional logic and legally-compliant e-signatures; encrypted document vault with expiry alerts (licenses, certifications), versioning, and permission-scoped access; clinical notes held in a segregated, field-encrypted domain with mandatory access logging.

## 5.15 Localization
Native Arabic RTL and English LTR with additional languages (French, Urdu); per-organization language configuration; content translation for services, products, and templates; regional date/time formats and Hijri display.

## 5.16 Integrations & Open Platform
Two-way Google/Outlook/Apple calendar sync; QuickBooks and Xero accounting sync; Mailchimp and social booking (Instagram/Facebook); organization-defined webhooks with retry; full REST API with OAuth 2.0, API keys, and rate limiting.

## 5.17 Security & Trust
Encryption at rest and in transit; strict multi-tenant isolation (row-level security on shared tier; dedicated isolated databases for Enterprise); 2FA; session and device management; append-only audit logs; configurable retention; PDPL data-subject export and deletion; automated backups with point-in-time recovery; in-Kingdom hosting options.

| Layer | Technology | Why |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Web frontend | **SvelteKit (SSR) + Tailwind CSS v4** | Fastest-loading premium UI in class; SEO-ready booking pages per tenant |

| Async processing | **Celery workers + beat** | ZATCA submissions, campaign sends, report generation, webhooks (outbox pattern) |
| Storage | **S3-compatible, per-tenant prefixes** | Documents, media, invoice XML archive |
| Payments | <p>**Moyasar**</p><p> (mada, Visa/MC, Apple Pay)</p> | Idempotent webhook processing; settlement reconciliation |

**Architecture principles:** API-first; tenant_id on every row with RLS enforcement; explicit state machines (never boolean flags) for bookings, payments, invoices, and subscriptions; immutable ledgers for money and stock; idempotency keys on every mutating endpoint; horizontal, stateless scaling.

# 7. Regulatory & Legal Position (KSA)
Namaa is engineered around — and differentiated by — the Saudi regulatory stack:

- **ZATCA (Fatoora):** Full Phase-2 integration capability (§5.8). We pursue certified-solution-provider status as a marketing asset, not just a feature.
- **PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law):** Consent records per customer per channel per purpose; data-subject export/deletion; processing registers; in-Kingdom residency options. Health-related notes are treated as sensitive data with field-level encryption and access logging.
- **E-signature validity:** Digital intake/consent signatures aligned with the Saudi Electronic Transactions Law.
- **Payments perimeter:** Namaa does not hold client funds — settlement flows through licensed gateways (Moyasar), keeping us outside SAMA licensing requirements at launch while we evaluate SoftPOS/aggregation authorization as a later strategic step.
- **Commercial posture:** Saudi LLC, MISA/CR registration, VAT registration, customer contracts with Saudi governing law, and clear SaaS terms (uptime SLA tiers, data ownership remains with the customer).
