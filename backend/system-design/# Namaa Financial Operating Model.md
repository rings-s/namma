# Namaa Financial Operating Model

## Revenue Architecture, Unit Economics, Forecasting & Capital Efficiency Strategy

**Version 1.0 — June 2026**

---

# Executive Summary

Namaa is designed as a multi-layer revenue platform rather than a single-product SaaS business.

While subscription revenue provides predictable recurring cash flows, long-term enterprise value is driven by embedded payments, communications infrastructure, AI services, and account expansion.

This creates a hybrid SaaS + Fintech + Revenue Infrastructure model with strong operating leverage and expanding customer lifetime value.

The financial objective is to build a capital-efficient company capable of achieving:

* Net Revenue Retention (NRR) above 120%
* Gross Margins above 80%
* CAC Payback below 9 months
* LTV/CAC above 5x
* Positive operating cash flow before Series A

---

# Financial Philosophy

## Core Principle

Customer growth drives Namaa growth.

Every customer expansion event should increase:

1. Subscription Revenue
2. Payment Revenue
3. Messaging Revenue
4. Intelligence Revenue

The result is revenue compounding without proportional customer acquisition costs.

# Long-Term Vision

Namaa is not being built as a software company.

It is being built as the operating infrastructure layer for Gulf service commerce.

As customers process more revenue through the platform, adopt additional modules, and expand into multiple locations, Namaa's revenue scales faster than customer acquisition alone.

The combination of recurring SaaS revenue, embedded fintech revenue, lifecycle automation revenue, and enterprise expansion creates a highly defensible business model with strong capital efficiency and long-term compounding economics.
